Frontalis

Forehead & Scalp
Origin
Galea aponeurotica (epicranial aponeurosis)
Insertion
Skin of forehead, eyebrows, root of nose
Action
Raises eyebrows, wrinkles forehead horizontally, assists in opening eyes wide
Nerve Innervation
Facial nerve (CN VII) — temporal branch
Aging Role
Chronic contraction creates permanent horizontal forehead lines; hypertonicity pulls scalp forward increasing tension throughout galea
Tension Shows As
Raised resting brow position, deep horizontal lines, scalp tightness, forehead always appears "braced"
Techniques
Deep Muscle WorkScalp ReleaseLymphatic (open)

Corrugator Supercilii

Glabella & Brow
Origin
Medial superciliary arch (frontal bone)
Insertion
Skin of medial eyebrow
Action
Draws eyebrow medially and downward — creates vertical glabellar frown lines
Nerve Innervation
Facial nerve (CN VII) — temporal branch
Aging Role
Primary driver of the "11" lines between brows; chronic frowning, screen use, and stress cause sustained hypertonicity
Tension Shows As
Vertical lines between brows; eyebrows drawn together at rest; client looks worried or angry even when relaxed
Techniques
Deep Muscle WorkCross-fiber FrictionBrow Sculpting

Procerus

Glabella & Brow
Origin
Fascia over nasal bone and upper lateral nasal cartilage
Insertion
Skin between eyebrows (lower forehead / glabella)
Action
Pulls medial brow inferiorly; creates horizontal lines at root of nose
Nerve Innervation
Facial nerve (CN VII) — temporal branch
Aging Role
Creates horizontal "bunny lines" across nose bridge; often contracted alongside corrugator in chronic frowning
Tension Shows As
Horizontal lines at root of nose; nose scrunching expression at rest; brow depression
Techniques
Deep Muscle WorkLight Cross-fiber

Orbicularis Oculi

Eye Region
Origin
Medial orbital margin, medial palpebral ligament, lacrimal bone
Insertion
Skin around orbital rim; lateral palpebral raphe
Action
Closes eyelid (palpebral = blinking; orbital = forced closure); compresses lacrimal sac
Nerve Innervation
Facial nerve (CN VII) — temporal and zygomatic branches
Aging Role
Lateral portion creates crow's feet with repeated squinting; orbital portion compresses lymphatics creating under-eye puffiness; palpebral laxity causes eyelid hooding
Tension Shows As
Crow's feet at outer corners; under-eye puffiness; eyelid appears heavy; fine radiating lines from lateral canthus
Techniques
Soft LymphaticGentle Cross-fiber (lateral only)Small Cup (lateral only)

Temporalis

Jaw & Temple
Origin
Temporal fossa (entire floor and deep surface of temporal fascia)
Insertion
Coronoid process and anterior ramus of mandible
Action
Elevates mandible; posterior fibers retract mandible; maintains jaw position at rest
Nerve Innervation
Trigeminal nerve (CN V) — mandibular division (V3), deep temporal nerves
Aging Role
Chronic clenching creates temple hollowing; contributes to lateral brow descent; restriction causes radiating head pain and jaw tension
Tension Shows As
Hollowed temples; temporal headache; tightness at side of head; upper jaw tension; earache without infection
Techniques
Deep Muscle WorkBuccal (for jaw)Lymphatic (close)

Zygomaticus Major

Cheek & Mid-Face
Origin
Lateral zygomatic bone (cheekbone)
Insertion
Modiolus (corner of mouth); orbicularis oris
Action
Draws angle of mouth superiorly and laterally — primary smiling muscle
Nerve Innervation
Facial nerve (CN VII) — zygomatic and buccal branches
Aging Role
Repeated contraction deepens nasolabial folds; loss of tone contributes to mid-face descent and flattening of cheek contour
Tension Shows As
Deep nasolabial folds; flat or descended cheek contour; cheek appears to drop below zygomatic arch
Techniques
Facial SculptingBuccalCupping

Zygomaticus Minor

Cheek & Mid-Face
Origin
Malar surface of zygomatic bone
Insertion
Skin of upper lip (lateral to philtrum)
Action
Elevates upper lip; contributes to nasolabial fold
Nerve Innervation
Facial nerve (CN VII) — zygomatic and buccal branches
Aging Role
Works alongside zygomaticus major in mid-face descent; contributes to nasolabial fold and upper lip thinning appearance
Tension Shows As
Deepening nasolabial fold; upper lip appears thinner; vertical lines beginning lateral to philtrum
Techniques
Facial SculptingBuccal

Levator Labii Superioris

Cheek & Mid-Face
Origin
Infraorbital margin of maxilla
Insertion
Skin of upper lip; alar cartilage of nose
Action
Elevates and everts upper lip; dilates nostril
Nerve Innervation
Facial nerve (CN VII) — zygomatic and buccal branches
Aging Role
Key driver of nasolabial fold deepening; contributes to "gummy smile"; chronic tension thins upper lip appearance
Tension Shows As
Deep nasolabial folds; upper lip appears thin; nostrils slightly flared at rest
Techniques
BuccalDeep Muscle WorkSculpting

Buccinator

Cheek — Primary Buccal Target
Origin
Alveolar processes of maxilla and mandible; pterygomandibular raphe
Insertion
Modiolus; blends with orbicularis oris
Action
Compresses cheeks against teeth; assists chewing and blowing; maintains food between molars
Nerve Innervation
Facial nerve (CN VII) — buccal branch
Aging Role
Loss of tone contributes to jowl formation and cheek laxity; hypertonicity from grinding creates tension lines; primary Buccal massage target
Tension Shows As
Puffiness at mid-cheek; loss of jawline definition; cheek feels thick and tough on palpation
Techniques
Buccal MassageFacial SculptingLymphatic

Masseter

Jaw — Most Commonly Hypertonic
Origin
Zygomatic arch and zygomatic process of maxilla
Insertion
Lateral surface and angle of mandible; coronoid process
Action
Elevates mandible (closes jaw); assists lateral jaw movement; deep portion retracts mandible
Nerve Innervation
Trigeminal nerve (CN V) — mandibular division (V3), masseteric nerve
Aging Role
Most commonly hypertonic facial muscle; grinding/clenching causes bulk hypertrophy that squares the jaw and creates jowl appearance; restricts lymphatic drainage from lower face
Tension Shows As
Squared or enlarged lower jaw; jowling; cheek puffiness; jaw soreness; temporal headaches; clicking or grinding sounds
Techniques
Deep Muscle WorkBuccal MassageLymphatic (close)

Orbicularis Oris

Mouth & Lower Face
Origin
No bone attachment — fibers from surrounding muscles (modiolus, buccinator)
Insertion
Skin and mucosa of lips; blends with surrounding muscles
Action
Closes and protrudes lips; shapes lips for speech, kissing, and expression
Nerve Innervation
Facial nerve (CN VII) — buccal branch
Aging Role
Chronic contraction creates perioral vertical lip lines ("smoker's lines"); loss of tone causes lip thinning and loss of definition at vermillion border
Tension Shows As
Vertical lines above and below lip border; lip appears thinner and less defined; pursed or pinched look at rest
Techniques
Buccal MassageDeep Muscle WorkCupping (perioral)

Depressor Anguli Oris

Mouth & Lower Face
Origin
Oblique line of mandible
Insertion
Modiolus; skin at angle of mouth
Action
Depresses corner of mouth — creates frowning expression
Nerve Innervation
Facial nerve (CN VII) — marginal mandibular branch
Aging Role
Hypertonicity causes persistent downturned mouth corners; primary contributor to marionette line formation
Tension Shows As
Downturned mouth corners at rest; marionette lines; resting frown expression; client looks sad or displeased
Techniques
Buccal MassageDeep Muscle WorkSculpting (lift)

Mentalis

Chin
Origin
Incisive fossa of mandible
Insertion
Skin of chin
Action
Elevates and protrudes lower lip; wrinkles chin skin
Nerve Innervation
Facial nerve (CN VII) — marginal mandibular branch
Aging Role
Hypertonicity creates "orange peel" or "cobblestone" chin dimpling; often overactive when orbicularis oris is weak
Tension Shows As
Dimpled or bumpy chin texture; puckered chin at rest; lower lip appears pushed forward
Techniques
Deep Muscle WorkBuccal (inferior)

Platysma

Neck — Critical Aging Muscle
Origin
Fascia over pectoralis major and deltoid (clavicular region)
Insertion
Base of mandible; skin of lower face and cheek; modiolus; corners of mouth
Action
Depresses mandible; depresses lower lip and corners of mouth; tenses skin of neck
Nerve Innervation
Facial nerve (CN VII) — cervical branch
Aging Role
CRITICAL — directly inserts into lower face. Laxity creates neck bands. Chronic downward pull contributes to jowling, mouth corner descent, and overall lower face sagging. Single most impactful neck muscle for facial rejuvenation.
Tension Shows As
Vertical neck bands (platysmal banding); jowling; downturned mouth corners; lower face drooping; "turkey neck" appearance
Techniques
Lymphatic (neck)SculptingDeep Neck Work

Sternocleidomastoid (SCM)

Neck — Lymphatic Gatekeeper
Origin
Manubrium of sternum (sternal head); medial clavicle (clavicular head)
Insertion
Mastoid process of temporal bone; lateral superior nuchal line of occipital bone
Action
Unilateral: rotates head to opposite side, lateral flexion to same side. Bilateral: neck flexion, assisted inspiration
Nerve Innervation
Accessory nerve (CN XI); cervical plexus (C2–C3)
Aging Role
Hypertonicity limits lymphatic drainage from the face — puffiness and fluid retention worsen when SCM is chronically tight. Affects ear, jaw, and temporal tension.
Tension Shows As
Face appears puffier than expected; poor lymphatic drainage despite treatment; jaw tension that doesn't resolve; earache; headache radiating from neck; restricted neck rotation
Techniques
Neck LymphaticDeep Neck ReleaseAlways address before facial drainage

Suboccipitals

Head / Neck — Hidden Forehead Driver
Muscles
Rectus capitis posterior major & minor; Obliquus capitis superior & inferior
Origin
Posterior atlas (C1) and axis (C2)
Insertion
Inferior nuchal line of occipital bone
Action
Extend and rotate head at atlanto-occipital and atlanto-axial joints; fine motor control of head position
Nerve Innervation
Suboccipital nerve (posterior ramus of C1)
Aging Role
Restriction here is often the hidden driver of chronic forehead tension — the suboccipital-frontal fascial chain runs continuously. Tight suboccipitals create compensatory frontalis hypertonicity.
Tension Shows As
Chronic forehead tension that doesn't respond to direct treatment; base-of-skull headaches; client always furrows brow; tension worsens after screen time
Techniques
Suboccipital ReleaseAddress before forehead work

Occipitalis

Scalp / Head
Origin
Lateral 2/3 of superior nuchal line; mastoid process of temporal bone
Insertion
Galea aponeurotica
Action
Retracts scalp posteriorly; works antagonistically with frontalis
Nerve Innervation
Facial nerve (CN VII) — posterior auricular branch
Aging Role
When occipitalis is tight, it limits galea mobility — contributing to scalp tightness that pulls on forehead skin, deepening forehead lines
Tension Shows As
Scalp that doesn't move freely; frontal headaches; forehead tension even when frontalis isn't contracted
Techniques
Scalp/Galea ReleaseAddress with Frontalis work

Trapezius (Upper Fibers)

Neck / Shoulder — Lymphatic Chain
Origin
External occipital protuberance; medial superior nuchal line; ligamentum nuchae; spinous processes C7–T12
Insertion
Lateral clavicle; acromion; spine of scapula
Action
Upper: elevates scapula, extends and laterally flexes head. Middle: retracts scapula. Lower: depresses scapula
Nerve Innervation
Accessory nerve (CN XI); cervical plexus (C3–C4)
Aging Role
Chronic upper trap tension restricts lymphatic drainage, compresses cervical lymph nodes, and perpetuates lower face puffiness and jowling that facial massage alone cannot resolve
Tension Shows As
Shoulders raised toward ears; facial puffiness that returns quickly after lymphatic drainage; persistent cheek heaviness; lateral neck tension
Techniques
Upper Trap ReleaseClavicular Lymph NodesNeck Release before drainage

Digastric

Submental / Jaw Floor
Origin
Posterior belly: mastoid notch of temporal bone. Anterior belly: digastric fossa of mandible
Insertion
Intermediate tendon attached to hyoid bone
Action
Opens jaw (depresses mandible); elevates hyoid; assists in swallowing
Nerve Innervation
Posterior belly: facial nerve (CN VII). Anterior belly: trigeminal nerve (V3) via mylohyoid nerve
Aging Role
Restriction under chin affects hyoid mobility, tensioning the entire floor of mouth — contributing to chin area descent, submental fullness, and loss of jawline definition
Tension Shows As
Double chin or submental fullness that persists; poor jawline definition; chin feels "heavy"; difficulty swallowing comfortably
Techniques
Submental ReleaseLymphaticDeep Neck Work

Pectoralis Major (Clavicular Head)

Chest — Posture & Fascial Chain
Origin
Medial clavicle; sternum; upper costal cartilages
Insertion
Greater tubercle of humerus
Action
Flexes and adducts humerus; medially rotates arm; assists forced inspiration
Nerve Innervation
Lateral and medial pectoral nerves (C5–T1)
Aging Role
Tight pectorals pull shoulders forward creating forward head posture — which increases load on posterior neck muscles, compresses anterior neck lymphatics, and accelerates lower face and neck descent via fascial tension chain
Tension Shows As
Forward head posture; anterior shoulder rounding; this position passively stretches and weakens platysma and lower face muscles, accelerating descent and banding
Techniques
Pec Stretch / ReleaseAddress posture chainDecolletage work

Treatment Sequences

Universal Rule: Open (Lymphatic) → Work (Targeted) → Close (Lymphatic)

Puffiness / Fluid Retention

under-eye bags, morning puffiness
Open
Lymphatic Drainage
Open lymph nodes: clavicle → neck → preauricular. Feather pressure.
Work
Soft Lymphatic
Maintain feather-light pressure throughout entire face.
Close
Light Sculpting + Effleurage
Gentle strokes to settle and smooth tissue.

Fine Lines — Dehydration

surface crepiness, texture lines
Open
Lymphatic Drainage
Clear lymphatic pathways first.
Work
Facial Cupping
Suction plumps tissue; draws nutrients to surface. Small cup around eyes, larger on cheeks.
Work 2
Traditional Soft Lymphatic
Alternate with cupping to maximize fluid movement.
Close
Lymphatic
Flush loosened congestion and debris.

Fine Lines — Expression / Muscle

forehead, frown lines, crow's feet, lip lines
Open
Lymphatic Drainage
Decongest before working muscles.
Work
Deep Facial Muscle Work
Frontalis (forehead), corrugator/procerus (glabella), orbicularis oculi (crow's feet).
Work 2
Buccal (lip lines)
Releases orbicularis oris and buccinator from inside.
Close
Lymphatic + Effleurage
Drain and calm post-deep work.

Smile Lines / Nasolabial Folds

lines running nose to mouth corners
Open
Lymphatic
Full face drainage sequence.
Work 1
Buccal — Levator Labii / Buccinator
Work muscles driving the fold from inside.
Work 2
Deep Muscle — Zygomaticus
External cross-fiber along the fold line.
Work 3
Sculpting
Lift tissue superiorly along fold.
Close
Cupping + Lymphatic
Suction along fold, then lymphatic flush.

Forehead Wrinkles

horizontal lines, raised brow patterns
Open
Lymphatic
Drain from forehead toward temporal lymph nodes.
Work 1
Deep Muscle — Frontalis
Slow, firm cross-fiber friction perpendicular to the lines.
Work 2
Scalp / Galea Release
Release galea aponeurotica — tightness here pulls forehead skin.
Work 3
Sculpting
Smooth downward fascial strokes along forehead.
Close
Lymphatic + Effleurage
Calm the tissue.

Crow's Feet

lateral eye lines, crepiness at outer corners
Open
Soft Lymphatic
Feather strokes outward from orbit toward preauricular nodes. Extremely light.
Work 1
Deep Muscle — Orb. Oculi
Gentle cross-fiber at lateral orbital rim ONLY. Never on orbital bone.
Work 2
Cupping — Small Cup
Very light suction laterally. Never over eyelid.
Close
Soft Lymphatic
Drain from lateral eye outward to preauricular.

Jowls / Loss of Definition

jowling, sagging cheeks, jawline
Open
Lymphatic Drainage
Decongest — you cannot lift congested tissue effectively.
Work 1
Buccal — Buccinator + Masseter
Release from inside; address bulk of masseter if hypertonic.
Work 2
Facial Sculpting
Work fascia along jawline and cheekbone superiorly.
Close
Lymphatic
Drain everything mobilized.

Jaw Tension / TMJ / Grinding

tight masseter, jaw pain, clenching
Open
Deep Muscle — Masseter + Temporalis
Start external before intraoral. Slow sustained pressure into muscle belly.
Work 1
Deep Muscle — SCM + Digastric
Release cervical connections pulling on jaw.
Work 2
Buccal Massage
Intraoral masseter and pterygoid release.
Close
Traditional Soft Lymphatic
Tissue will be tender — parasympathetic finish essential.

Deep Wrinkles / Volume Loss

nasolabial folds, marionette lines, lip lines
Open
Lymphatic Drainage
Full lymphatic clearing sequence.
Work 1
Buccal Massage
Work levator labii, buccinator, orbicularis oris intraorally.
Work 2
Deep Facial Muscle Work
External work on masseter, zygomaticus major/minor.
Work 3
Facial Sculpting
Lift and reposition fascia toward origin points.
Close
Cupping + Lymphatic
Cupping for collagen stimulus, then lymphatic flush.

General Aging / Rejuvenation

combination of laxity, dullness, mild tension
Open
Traditional Soft Lymphatic
Most versatile and universally tolerated opening.
Work 1
Facial Sculpting
Address fascia and overall lift.
Work 2
Cupping (cheeks + forehead)
Collagen stimulus without aggression.
Work 3
Buccal (if appropriate)
Deepen results on folds and lip area.
Close
Lymphatic
Full flush to finish.

Sensitive / Reactive Skin

rosacea, post-procedure, compromised barrier
Only
Traditional Soft Lymphatic
No cupping, no deep work, no Buccal. Nothing stimulating.
Goal
Reduce, calm, support
Keep everything parasympathetic. Do not stimulate.
